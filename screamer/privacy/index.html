Privacy Policy

This page is used to inform users of Screamer regarding my policies for the collection, use, and disclosure of Personal Information, should anyone decide to use this Service.

To avoid ambiguity, 'Screamer' here is the Android app developed and released by Paul Guest.

If you choose to use Screamer, then you agree to the collection and use of information in relation to this policy. The Personal Information that I collect is used for providing and improving the Service. I will not use or share your information with anyone except as described in this Privacy Policy.

Information Collection and Use

Screamer has been designed in such a way as to operate without the collection or storage of your personal information. There are, however, cases where your information may be disclosed as part of the expected operation of the service. The service does not use information for any reason beyond those described below.

System Permissions

Depending on your particular device and operation system version, Screamer may require your permission to perform certain functions, which would allow access to some data that may be considered personal information.

  • Physical Activity
    • The service can make use of your device's internal Step Counter hardware in order to improve movement detection. Should your device request permission to access 'Physical Activity', 'Acitivity Recognition', or some similar wording, it is only for the purpose of accessing the Step Counter. Step Counter events are consumed on-the-spot during an active service period; and are thus not stored, collected or shared.

Lockscreen Contact Information

Should you choose to enable the function to show your contact information on the lockscreen, you will be asked to supply some personal data. This function is disabled by default, and the choice is yours to opt in. The information that you provide to this function is encrypted before storage on your device, and is never accessed for any purpose other than:

  • when you update that information on the preferences page;
  • when the lockscreen notification appears after a siren has been sounding.

Log Data

In the case of an error in the app, the operating system may collect data and information (through third-party products) related to the error. This data may include information such as your Internet Protocol (IP) address, device name, operating system version, the configuration of the app, the time and date, and other statistics. Should the collected information inadvertantly contain any private data, that data will be anonymised immediately.

In addition to this system-collected log data, Screamer keeps an internal log of data related to a small number of recent 'active schedules'. These logs will never hold any personal information, and can only be shared by choosing to do so when contacting the developer via the app.

Email Contact

When you contact the developer from the 'contact' link within the app, you have the option to attach the internal logs mentioned above, and a rundown of the app's settings and allowed permissions. The attachment will include anonymous information about your device's manufacturer, model name and operating system version; and will not include any of your private information.

Your name and email address will not be collected or shared for any other purpose than to reply to your contact.
